WMV-9 Video Encoding

WMV-9 video coding is Microsoft's offering, capable of efficient coding of HD video

WMV-9 (Windows Media Video - 9 or VC-9) is a new video codec developed by Microsoft and incorporates the VC-9 codec.  WMV-9 is claimed to ensure unmatched video quality at any bit rate and a WMV-9 video file is typically only half the size of an MPEG-4 file of comparable quality.

Windows Media Video 9 Professional (WMV-9 Pro) uses profiles at higher bit rates and resolutions, enabling storage and delivery of HD video at 720p resolution (1280 x 720 pixels per frame), with 5.1 digital discrete channel surround-sound, on a single standard DVD. 

WMV-9 can use variable bit rate encoding for optimal quality at a lower average file size. WMV-9 also supports a peak-constrained VBR mode, enabling the user to specify the maximum bit rate allowed, and is therefore suitable for low-bit-rate connections and devices such as DVD players.

WMV HD

WMV HD brings a whole new level of entertainment to high definition capable Windows XP-based PCs. With support for 720p and 1080p video WMV HD is capable of producing 6 times the quality of today's standard definition DVDs. But the bit rate needed is two to three times lower than MPEG-2 for equivalent quality.

If the decision to include WMV HD as one of the mandatory video codecs for HD DVD is confirmed, then it will allow a high quality full length movie in 1080 line HD video to be stored on a single layer HD DVD and leave room for extras.  Bit rates are likely to be less than 10 Mb/s and possibly only 8 Mb/s but still offer high quality, equivalent to MPEG-2 at 20 to 25 Mb/s.

WMV HD requires a lot of processing power to encode and decode.  A software decoder needs a Windows PC with a 2.4 GHz processor for 720p video and a 3 GHz processor for 1080p with 5.1 channel surround sound.

For more information on WMV-9 and WMV HD see Microsoft, where sample WMV HD clips can be downloaded.
